IT Project Team56 OOSD-Questify : 24-Aug-2025 Team Meeting Notes

Created by Chang Chang on Sep 21, 2025

KEY action: Making changes to the core idea of the whole design

Reason:

The initial design idea was to meet the students‚Äô needs (as they are the majority of users of our product): providing a question bank to practice more questions. However, this contradicts the main expectation of the client. As the lecturer, her real goal is to get students more engaged in the subject throughout the entire semester. From her perspective, most students are not consistently following the weekly content. Instead, they tend to cram before the final exam, rushing through past papers and exam-style questions. This issue was also reflected in the results of the Google Form survey. In this situation, students are primarily memorising ‚Äúhow to solve exam-type questions‚Äù rather than gaining a thorough understanding of the subject content. Having only a question bank would actually reinforce this pattern. Therefore, we decided to redesign the system in a way that motivates students to think critically and develop a deeper understanding of the content.

New Design:

In the new design, the focus shifts from a static question bank to a student-driven question creation system. Students are encouraged to design their own questions based on different topics, provide answers, and include detailed explanations. By creating high-quality questions to challenge their peers, students engage more deeply with the subject material and strengthen their own understanding.

Peers can then attempt these student-created questions, give ratings on their quality, and leave comments for discussion. This process transforms practicing questions into an interactive learning experience‚Äîstudents not only solve problems but also critically evaluate whether the questions are correct, interesting, and novel.

To further motivate participation, a leaderboard highlights both the popularity and quality of questions, as well as students‚Äô activity in creating and solving them. We also plan to consult the client about offering small weekly or monthly rewards (such as lecture announcements or small gifts like chocolates) for top contributors, fostering healthy competition and sustained engagement throughout the semester.
