IT Project Team56 OOSD-Questify : Non-Functional Requirement

Created by Chang Chang on Sep 21, 2025

1.1 Performance Requirements

NFR-001: Response Time: The system shall meet specific response time requirements for optimal user experience.

NFR-001.1: The system shall respond to user interactions within 2 seconds for 95% of operations
NFR-001.2: Database queries shall complete within 1 second for standard operations
NFR-001.3: Page loading shall complete within 3 seconds on standard broadband connections
NFR-001.4: System shall maintain response times under peak load conditions NFR-001.5: Open saved questions shall take less than 2 seconds.
NFR-001.6: Switch pages: Moving between pages finishes in less than 1 second after click. NFR-001.7: Upload a question is instant and are visible to others in less than 60 seconds.
NFR-001.8: Comments show up: New comments are visible to others in less than 60 seconds. (post then check with other accounts)

NFR-002: Throughput and Capacity: The system shall support concurrent operations without performance degradation. NFR-002.1: The system shall support 100 concurrent scoring sessions simultaneously
NFR-002.2: The system shall handle 500 concurrent user sessions during peak usage periods
NFR-002.3: System shall maintain performance with 5 concurrent administrative operations

NFR-003: Scalability: The system architecture shall support growth and expansion requirements.

NFR-003.1: System shall support horizontal scaling to accommodate user growth
NFR-003.2: Database design shall support partitioning and sharing for large datasets
NFR-003.3: Application shall support load balancing across multiple servers
NFR-003.4: Storage systems shall scale to support increasing data volumes NFR-003.5: Concurrent users: Works for 500 users at once with acceptable speed. Prove with load tests. NFR-003.6: Data size: Can store 100,000 attempts over 1 year. Move data older than 1 year(based it on year group) to archive storage. Test with seeded data.

1.2 Reliability and Availability

NFR-004: System Availability: The system shall maintain high availability during operational periods.

NFR-004.1: The system shall maintain 99.9% uptime(8 AM - 11 PM)
NFR-004.2: Planned maintenance windows shall not exceed 4 hours per month
NFR-004.3: System shall recover from component failures within 15 minutes
NFR-004.4: Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF) shall exceed 720 hours
NFR-004.5: System shall provide graceful degradation during partial failures = NFR-004.6: Recovery: Fix major outages in less than an hour. Do incremental onsite data recovery so users can access the website whenever. NFR-004.7: Low failures: For login, save/submit, and search, less than 1% of requests fail.

NFR-005: Data Integrity and Backup: The system shall ensure data protection and recovery capabilities.

NFR-005.1: The system shall perform automated daily backups of all data
NFR-005.2: Backup retention shall be maintained for 12 months minimum
NFR-005.3: Data corruption incidents shall not exceed 0.01% of all transactions
NFR-005.4: System shall provide point-in-time recovery capabilities
NFR-005.5: Backup verification shall be performed weekly to ensure data integrity

NFR-006: Error Handling and Recovery: The system shall handle errors gracefully and provide recovery mechanisms.

NFR-006.1: The system shall display user-friendly error messages with corrective guidance
NFR-006.2: System shall log all errors with sufficient detail for troubleshooting
NFR-006.3: Critical errors shall trigger automatic notifications to administrators
NFR-006.4: System shall provide rollback capabilities for failed operations
NFR-006.5: Session recovery shall be available for interrupted scoring sessions

1.3 Security Requirements

NFR-007: Authentication and Authorization Security: The system shall implement robust security measures for user access control.

NFR-007.1: All user passwords shall be hashed using bcrypt or equivalent algorithms
NFR-007.2: The system shall support multi-factor authentication for administrative accounts
NFR-007.3: Role-based access control shall be enforced at all system layers
NFR-007.4: Account lockout policies shall prevent brute force attacks NFR-007.5: Security checks: No high/critical findings in quarterly scans
NFR-007.6.1: Allow sign-up only with university email domain (@unimelb.au) and only allow users with this type of ID to signup.
NFR-007.6.2: Remove or deactivate accounts for past students at the end of the academic year.
Verify: try sign-up with non-uni email; check auto-deactivation. NFR-007.7: Rate limits: After 5 failed logins in 1 minute, slow down with a short lock (e.g., 5 minutes)

NFR-008: Data Protection: The system shall protect sensitive data through encryption and secure handling. NFR-008.1: All data transmission shall be encrypted using TLS 1.3 or higher
NFR-008.2: Sensitive data at rest shall be encrypted using AES-256 encryption
NFR-008.3: Database connections shall use encrypted protocols
NFR-008.4: Personal identifiable information shall be masked in logs and reports
NFR-008.5: Data anonymization shall be available for research and analytics purposes NFR-008.6: Privacy & consent: Collect only needed data (e.g., student ID/email). Show a consent notice at sign-up. Delete personal data when the student leaves, or after 1 year if not required.

NFR-009: Audit and Compliance: The system shall maintain comprehensive audit trails for security and compliance. NFR-009.1: The system shall log all user authentication and authorization events
NFR-009.2: All data access and modification operations shall be audited
NFR-009.3: Audit logs shall be tamper-proof and securely stored

1.4 Usability Requirements

NFR-010: User Interface Design: The system shall provide intuitive and accessible user interfaces.

NFR-010.1: Interface design shall follow established usability principles and guidelines
NFR-010.2: New users shall complete basic scoring tasks within 10 minutes of initial training
NFR-010.3: System shall provide contextual help and tooltips for all functions
NFR-010.4: Error messages shall be clear and provide actionable guidance
NFR-010.5: Interface shall comply with WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards NFR-010.6: New user flow: A new user can sign up and try the first question in less than 5 minutes. Check with user test. NFR-010.7: Accessibility: Keyboard works for login, attempt, save, search, and there is a clear color contrast so it is easy to click and distinguish buttons and pages. Check with an audit tool + manual check. NFR-010.8: Few clicks: From any page, core actions (attempt, save, search) are â‰¤ 3 clicks away. Check with click-path review.

NFR-011: Device Compatibility and Responsiveness: The system shall function effectively across different devices and screen sizes.

NFR-011.1: System shall provide responsive design for desktop, tablet, and mobile devices
NFR-011.2: Interface shall adapt to screen sizes ranging from 320px to 2560px width
NFR-011.3: Touch interface shall be optimized for tablet and mobile interactions
NFR-011.4: System shall maintain full functionality on supported mobile devices
NFR-011.5: Cross-device synchronization shall maintain user session state

NFR-012: Browser and Platform Support: The system shall support modern browsers and operating systems.

NFR-012.1: System shall support Chrome 90+, Firefox 88+, Safari 14+, and Edge 90+
NFR-012.2: JavaScript shall be required for full functionality
NFR-012.3: System shall degrade gracefully when advanced features are unavailable
NFR-012.4: Mobile browsers shall support core system functionality
NFR-012.5: System shall provide consistent experience across supported platforms
