Questions to Ask To Client on Week 2(1st meeting):

  1. What is the primary goal of the revamp? (Would you please specify more for the word “interesting”?)

    1. The goal is to explore new and engaging ways for students to learn, moving beyond traditional formats. For example, we previously used a game as a project. While that was fun, we’re now questioning whether that’s the most effective method. Could there be a more competitive, interactive, or educationally valuable tool?
      The format doesn’t matter (website, app, chatbot, etc.)—the focus is on creating tools that actually help students learn in a more interesting and meaningful way.

  2. Is there any problem that this subject is particularly facing? Is there any feedback for improvements from past students?

    1. One recurring issue is student engagement.
      A potential way to gather better feedback is to attend one of the lectures and distribute a survey asking: “How can we help you feel more engaged using GenAI?”  For example, students could learn the value of OOSD by building an AI chatbot themselves, gaining hands-on experience with both the technology and the design principles behind it.

  3. What is the purpose of using GenAI? (Project name states using GenAI, wh)

    1. Primarily because it's cutting-edge technology that aligns with current industry trends. The goal is to integrate GenAI not just for novelty, but to enhance the learning experience and show its real-world applications.

  4. When you mention a “personal tutor,” what features do you imagine it having?

    1. The idea is not necessarily a literal tutor, but any AI-driven tool that increases student engagement. This could include interactive feedback, instant answers, concept explanations, code review suggestions, or progress tracking.

  5. Would you prefer GenAI to be integrated into existing platforms (e.g. Canvas, Gradescope), or can it be a separate tool or chatbot?

    1. Does not matter

  6. Would you like us to import from existing AI like chat gpt or just through libraries like language models? What is recommended?

    1. Does not matter
