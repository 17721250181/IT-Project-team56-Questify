IT Project Team56 OOSD-Questify : 2025-09-18 Client Meeting notes

Created by Bill Park, last modified on Sep 26, 2025

1. Client Feedback

  • The client was pleased with our overall concept and direction.

  • Shanika raised a concern regarding one aspect of our design:

    • Students are less likely to remember material by week.

    • Students are more likely to recall and engage with material by topic.

2. Our Initial Assumption

  • We designed the system so that students could adjust question types based on weeks.

  • Rationale:

    • Academic milestones (e.g., mid-semester exams, final exams, assignment deadlines) are tied to specific weeks.

    • A week-based structure seemed logical for aligning study with these milestones.

3. Key Insight from Client

  • Students prioritize topics rather than weeks when revising and engaging with material.

  • Motivation is driven by topic-focused learning (e.g., revisiting areas they struggle with).

  • Weeks may not be a meaningful reference point for students outside of fixed deadlines.

4. Agreed Design Modification

  • Shift the system from a week-based adjustment model to a topic-based model.

  • Students will now be able to:

    • Adjust question types based on specific topics.

    • Focus on areas they want to revisit or strengthen.

    • Engage with the system in a way that feels more intuitive and relevant.

5. Expected Benefits

  • Improves alignment with how students naturally recall and engage with course content.

  • Enhances flexibility for students to personalize their study experience.

  • Supports deeper learning by allowing repeated practice of challenging topics.
